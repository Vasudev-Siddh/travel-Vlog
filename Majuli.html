Majuli, Assam


Embark on a journey to Majuli, the largest river island in the world, nestled in the Brahmaputra River in Assam, India. Join us on a virtual tour through the serene landscapes, vibrant culture, and unique traditions that make Majuli a haven for nature enthusiasts and seekers of spiritual experiences.

Majuli Overview

Discover the unique features and cultural richness of Majuli:

  • Satras of Majuli: Explore the Satras, traditional Assamese monastic institutions preserving the classical dance form of Sattriya.
  • Molai Forest: Visit the Molai Forest, a man-made forest created by environmentalist Jadav Payeng.
  • Kamalabari Satra: Immerse yourself in the cultural activities at Kamalabari Satra, one of the prominent Satras in Majuli.

Cultural and Spiritual Retreats


Immerse yourself in the cultural and spiritual experiences of Majuli:

  • Auniati Satra: Witness the Auniati Satra, known for its artifacts, masks, and cultural performances.
  • Samaguri Satra: Explore Samaguri Satra, where traditional masks for the Bhaona performances are crafted.
  • Natun Samaguri Satra: Experience the tranquility of Natun Samaguri Satra, known for its serene surroundings.

Nature and Wildlife

Experience the natural beauty and wildlife in and around Majuli:

  • Bird Watching: Majuli is a paradise for bird watchers, with a variety of migratory and resident birds.
  • Brahmaputra River Cruise: Take a boat cruise on the Brahmaputra to appreciate the scenic beauty of Majuli.
  • Dolphin Watching: Go dolphin watching in the Brahmaputra River for a unique experience.

Local Cuisine


Savor the flavors of Majuli's unique cuisine:

Try local dishes like Assam Laksa, Pitha, and Bamboo Shoot Curry, and experience the traditional Assamese culinary delights.

What to Wear and Pack

Plan your attire and essentials for a comfortable stay in Majuli:

  • Comfortable Clothing: Pack light, comfortable clothing suitable for the tropical climate.
  • Mosquito Repellent: Due to the island's location, carry mosquito repellent to prevent insect bites.
  • Camera: Capture the cultural richness and natural beauty of Majuli.

How to Reach Majuli


Plan your journey to Majuli with these transportation options:

  • By Ferry: Take a ferry from Jorhat to Majuli, which is a scenic and popular mode of transportation.
  • By Road: Majuli is well-connected by road, and buses and taxis are available from Jorhat.
  • By Air: The nearest airport is Jorhat Airport, and you can reach Majuli by road and ferry.

Responsible Travel Tips


Contribute to the preservation of Majuli's beauty with these responsible travel tips:

  • Respect Local Traditions: Follow the cultural norms and show respect when visiting Satras and local communities.
  • Minimize Environmental Impact: Dispose of waste responsibly and avoid littering in natural areas.
  • Support Local Communities: Choose local accommodations and support community-based tourism initiatives.
