Kaziranga National Park, Assam


Embark on a journey to Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the iconic one-horned Indian rhinoceros. Join us on a virtual safari through the lush landscapes, diverse wildlife, and conservation efforts that make Kaziranga a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.

Kaziranga National Park Overview

Discover the unique features and biodiversity of Kaziranga:

  • One-Horned Rhinoceros: Kaziranga is famous for its population of the one-horned Indian rhinoceros, a conservation success story.
  • Biodiversity: Explore the rich biodiversity, including tigers, elephants, wild water buffaloes, and a variety of bird species.
  • Unique Ecosystem: The park features wetlands, grasslands, and dense forests, creating a diverse and thriving ecosystem.

Wildlife Safari


Experience the thrill of a wildlife safari in Kaziranga:

  • Jeep Safari: Take a jeep safari for a closer look at the wildlife, including rhinoceros, elephants, and deer.
  • Elephant Safari: Enjoy a unique perspective from the back of an elephant, allowing you to traverse areas not accessible by vehicles.
  • Bird Watching: Kaziranga is a paradise for bird watchers, with numerous migratory and resident bird species.

Conservation Efforts

Learn about the conservation initiatives that make Kaziranga a success story:

  • Anti-Poaching Measures: Kaziranga is known for its strong anti-poaching efforts to protect endangered species.
  • Community Involvement: Explore how local communities are actively involved in conservation and sustainable tourism.
  • Education Programs: Discover the park's educational initiatives aimed at raising awareness about wildlife conservation.

Best Time to Visit


Plan your visit to Kaziranga National Park at the optimal time:

  • November to April: The park is open during these months, with the winter season offering pleasant weather and excellent wildlife sightings.
  • Monsoon Closure: Kaziranga is closed to visitors during the monsoon season (May to October) due to heavy rainfall.

Accommodations

Explore accommodation options for a comfortable stay near Kaziranga:

  • Resorts: Stay in wildlife resorts and lodges offering a blend of comfort and proximity to nature.
  • Forest Rest Houses: Experience the thrill of staying within the park in forest rest houses, subject to availability.
  • Local Homestays: Immerse yourself in the local culture by opting for homestays in nearby villages.

What to Pack


Ensure a memorable and comfortable safari experience by packing the essentials:

  • Binoculars: Enhance your wildlife viewing experience with a pair of binoculars.
  • Camera and Zoom Lens: Capture the beauty of Kaziranga and its inhabitants with a good camera and zoom lens.
  • Comfortable Clothing: Dress in neutral colors for safaris and wear comfortable shoes.
  • Sunscreen and Hat: Protect yourself from the sun during outdoor activities.

Responsible Tourism


Contribute to the conservation of Kaziranga and practice responsible tourism:

  • Follow Park Rules: Adhere to the rules and guidelines set by the national park for a responsible safari experience.
  • Support Conservation Initiatives: Contribute to conservation efforts by supporting local initiatives and organizations.
  • Respect Wildlife: Observe animals from a safe distance and avoid disturbing their natural behavior.
