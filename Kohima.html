Kohima, Nagaland


Embark on a journey to Kohima, the capital city of Nagaland in Northeast India. Join us on a virtual tour through the serene landscapes, rich Naga culture, and historical significance that make Kohima a unique destination in the heart of the Naga Hills.

Kohima Overview

Discover the unique features and cultural richness of Kohima:

  • War Cemetery: Pay homage at the War Cemetery, a memorial for the soldiers who lost their lives during World War II.
  • Kohima State Museum: Explore the Kohima State Museum, showcasing the diverse culture and heritage of Nagaland.
  • Kisama Heritage Village: Immerse yourself in Naga traditions at the Kisama Heritage Village.

Cultural and Historical Attractions


Immerse yourself in the cultural and historical richness of Kohima:

  • Kohima Cathedral: Visit the Kohima Cathedral, a beautiful Catholic church with panoramic views of the city.
  • Dzukou Valley: Explore the picturesque Dzukou Valley, known for its vibrant flowers and trekking trails.
  • Japfu Peak: Trek to Japfu Peak for stunning views of the surrounding hills and landscapes.

Nature and Adventure


Experience the natural beauty and adventure in and around Kohima:

  • Kohima Zoo: Discover the biodiversity of Nagaland at the Kohima Zoo.
  • Khonoma Village: Visit the eco-friendly Khonoma Village, known for its conservation efforts and traditional practices.
  • Trekking in Nagaland: For adventure seekers, explore the various trekking options amidst the lush greenery.

Local Cuisine

Savor the flavors of Kohima's unique cuisine:

Try local dishes like Smoked Pork with Bamboo Shoot, Bamboo Steamed Fish, and Axone (fermented soybean dish).

What to Wear and Pack


Plan your attire and essentials for a comfortable stay in Kohima:

  • Comfortable Clothing: Pack comfortable and modest clothing, respecting the local culture.
  • Walking Shoes: Wear comfortable walking shoes for exploring the hilly terrain.
  • Rain Gear: Due to the region's unpredictable weather, carry a rain jacket and umbrella.
  • Camera: Capture the cultural richness and scenic landscapes of Kohima.

How to Reach Kohima

Plan your journey to Kohima with these transportation options:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Dimapur Airport, and taxis or buses are available for the journey to Kohima.
  • By Road: Kohima is well-connected by road, and buses and taxis operate from major cities.
  • By Train: The nearest railway station is Dimapur, and you can continue your journey to Kohima by road.

Responsible Travel Tips


Contribute to the preservation of Kohima's beauty with these responsible travel tips:

  • Respect Local Traditions: Learn about and respect the cultural practices of the Naga tribes.
  • Support Local Artisans: Purchase local handicrafts and support the livelihoods of local artisans.
  • Minimize Environmental Impact: Practice responsible tourism by minimizing waste and respecting nature.
