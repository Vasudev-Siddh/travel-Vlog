Meghalaya


Embark on a journey to Meghalaya, a northeastern state in India known for its lush landscapes, cascading waterfalls, and vibrant culture. Join us on a virtual tour through the picturesque hills, living root bridges, and cultural gems that make Meghalaya a hidden paradise for nature lovers and explorers.

Meghalaya Overview

Explore the unique features and cultural richness of Meghalaya:

  • Abundant Waterfalls: Discover the numerous waterfalls, including the famous Nohkalikai Falls, cascading down the hills.
  • Living Root Bridges: Witness the engineering marvels of living root bridges woven by the local Khasi and Jaintia tribes.
  • Cherrapunji: Visit one of the wettest places on Earth, known for its lush greenery and scenic beauty.

Key Attractions in Meghalaya


Immerse yourself in the beauty of Meghalaya's attractions:

  • Mawlynnong: Explore the cleanest village in Asia, known for its living root bridges and community-based eco-tourism.
  • Dawki River: Experience the crystal-clear waters of the Dawki River and indulge in boating with a surreal view.
  • Shillong: Visit the capital city, Shillong, known for its pleasant climate, vibrant markets, and colonial architecture.

Adventure and Nature Exploration

Thrill-seekers, rejoice! Meghalaya offers a range of adventure activities:

  • Caving: Explore the mesmerizing caves, including the Mawsmai Cave and the Siju-Dobkhakol Chibe Nala Cave.
  • Trekking: Trek to the Double-Decker Living Root Bridge in Nongriat for a rewarding adventure.
  • Boating at Umiam Lake: Enjoy a peaceful boat ride amidst the scenic beauty of Umiam Lake.

Cuisine and Local Delicacies


Savor the flavors of Meghalaya's unique cuisine:

Try local dishes like Jadoh, a traditional Khasi dish, and indulge in smoked pork with bamboo shoot, a delicacy in the region.

What to Wear and Pack

Plan your attire and essentials for a comfortable stay in Meghalaya:

  • Comfortable Clothing: Pack light, comfortable clothing suitable for varying temperatures.
  • Rain Gear: Given the region's frequent rainfall, carry a rain jacket and waterproof footwear.
  • Hiking Shoes: Wear sturdy hiking shoes for trekking and exploring natural trails.
  • Camera: Capture the breathtaking landscapes and cultural moments of Meghalaya.

How to Reach Meghalaya


Plan your journey to Meghalaya with these transportation options:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Shillong Airport, and Guwahati Airport is also well-connected.
  • By Road: Meghalaya is accessible by road, and buses and taxis are available from major cities.
  • By Train: The nearest railway station is in Guwahati, and you can continue your journey by road.

Responsible Travel Tips


Contribute to the preservation of Meghalaya's beauty with these responsible travel tips:

  • Respect Local Customs: Learn about and respect the traditions and customs of the local tribes.
  • Minimize Environmental Impact: Follow Leave No Trace principles and avoid littering.
  • Support Local Communities: Choose local accommodations and support community-based tourism initiatives.
